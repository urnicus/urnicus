## Eligibility Requirements

Illinois restricts voter data access to specific qualified requestors for approved purposes.

### Who Can Access

**Registered Political Committees**
- Must be registered as a political committee under Illinois law
- Political committee definition: Generally requires accepting or spending $5,000 or more
- Source: [10 ILCS 5/9-1.8](https://www.ilga.gov/Documents/legislation/ilcs/documents/001000050K9-1.8.htm)

**For Limited Public Access**
- General public may request redacted voter data during certain periods
- Public access excludes phone numbers, SSNs, street numbers, birth dates, and other sensitive information
- Public access is **not available during the 27 days immediately preceding any election**
- Source: 10 ILCS 5/1A-25(c)

### Permitted Uses

- Elections-related activities (political committees)
- Political activities (political committees)
- Bona fide political purposes

### Prohibited Uses

- Commercial solicitation
- Other business purposes

Source: 10 ICLS 5/4-8, 5-7, 6-35

## Voter List

Illinois provides voter registration data through the State Board of Elections with different data fields based on requestor type.

### How to Access

1. Visit the [Illinois Computerized Voter Data page](https://www.elections.il.gov/VotingAndRegistrationSystems/ComputerizedVoterData.aspx)
2. Download the appropriate application form (PDF link on page)
3. Confirm you meet eligibility requirements (political committee or public requestor)
4. Complete the application
5. Submit with payment ($500 for political committees)
6. Receive voter data after processing

### What's Included

The data included varies by requestor type:

**Political Committees**: Full voter registration data
**Public Requestors**: Redacted data (phone numbers, SSNs, street numbers, birth dates removed)

### Blackout Period

> **Important Restriction:**
>
> The statewide voter registration list is **not available to the public during the 27 days immediately preceding any election**.
>
> This blackout period does not apply to political committees accessing data for bona fide political purposes.

## Voter History

Illinois provides voter history in two different file format options.

### Format Options

**Option 1: Last Fifteen Elections as Columns**
- Voter history for the last 15 elections included as columns in the voter file
- Single integrated file
- Easier to use for analysis

**Option 2: Multiple CSVs Requiring Join**
- Voter registration list as one CSV
- Voter history as separate CSV file(s)
- Requires joining files using voter ID
- More flexible for custom analysis

### Historical Coverage

Illinois provides voter history for the **last 15 elections**, offering substantial historical voting behavior data.

## Cost

Illinois charges **$500** for statewide voter registration data with voter history for political committees.

Public requestors may face different pricing; contact the State Board of Elections for public access pricing.

## Restrictions and Permitted Uses

> **Legal Requirements:**
>
> - Voter data is available to registered political committees for bona fide political purposes
> - Use for commercial solicitation or business purposes is prohibited (10 ICLS 5/4-8, 5-7, 6-35)
> - Public access is limited and excludes sensitive data fields
> - Public access is blocked during the 27-day pre-election blackout period
> - Data is also available for limited purposes under the National Voter Registration Act (52 USC 20507)

## Technical Notes

### Political Committee Registration

To qualify as a political committee in Illinois:
- Must register under Illinois campaign finance laws
- Generally requires accepting or spending $5,000 or more
- Review [10 ILCS 5/9-1.8](https://www.ilga.gov/Documents/legislation/ilcs/documents/001000050K9-1.8.htm) for complete definition

### File Format Choice

Consider which voter history format best suits your needs:
- **Integrated (15 elections as columns)**: Easier for immediate analysis
- **Separate CSVs**: More flexible, requires data joining skills

### Pre-Election Blackout

Plan around the 27-day blackout period if you're a public requestor:
- Calculate 27 days before any election in Illinois
- Submit requests outside this window
- Political committees are exempt from this restriction
